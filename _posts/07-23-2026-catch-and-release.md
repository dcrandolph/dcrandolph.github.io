We sit on the floor, legs out, across the room. 

"Are you ready?"

I roll the ball. It makes its way to him, and his careful tiny hands scoop it up. We laugh and clap and he tries to roll it back. Not quite, but better. Better every time. There are lots of other times. 

Now he is bigger. Now we bounce the ball and almost catch it. Better every time. 

Now we throw. A gentle arc through the living room sky, a catch, we cheer and laugh and do it all again.

Forever is real and right here somehow. He is a baby and a little kid and maybe also a grandpa playing catch with his grandson and on into always, right now. All of math and physics and aeronautics in the path and spin and curve of the ball. 

The True things are simple, but are so full of compressed Mystery that when you see them truly you will spin off into the sky forever. 

Lies are complicated. And empty. There are no buzzwords or secrets, which amount to the same thing, in a game of catch. If it requires a guide or a paywall, there is no *there* there. Scratch the surface, and find nothing.

Just catch the ball. Now throw it back. The everything inside will address anything outside. Are you ready?
